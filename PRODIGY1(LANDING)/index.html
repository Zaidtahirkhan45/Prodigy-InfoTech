FOLLOW THE

WILDLIFE

Wildlife refers to all living organisms that are not domesticated by humans,
encompassing a vast array of flora and fauna found in natural habitats such as forests, 
deserts, grasslands, oceans, and rivers. It includes mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, 
fish, insects, and plants.

About

Wildlife showcases extraordinary adaptations for survival. 
The immortal jellyfish can revert to its juvenile form indefinitely.
Mimic octopuses imitate other animals to evade predators, 
while Malaysian a ants self-destruct to protect their colony.
Sea cucumbers expel and regenerate organs defensively,and pistol
shrimp create shockwaves with their claws. Dung beetles navigate 
using the Milky Way,and "zombie fungi" control ants' behavior.
Hairy frogs produce claws by breaking their own bones,narwhals 
use sensitive tusks for navigation, and American alligators have 
powerful immune systems.
